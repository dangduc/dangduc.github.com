Not a Love Story

Saturday June 11, 2011

So I re-watched 500 Days of Summer. I don’t typically re-watch movies, but I did this one. The story line cleverly jumps backwards and forwards in time, telling the progression of Tom’s romance with Summer. I say Tom’s, because the story is shown through his perspective. We garner glimpses of Summer’s state of mind through Tom. I say “cleverly”, but I cannot substantiate that claim (because I don’t feel like it). The visual cue cards in the form of numbered days helps to alleviate confusion on where a scene is in time sequence. And its very cute.

The opening scene begins near the end. Ms. Deschanel’s character Summer and Gordon-Levitt’s Tom sit on a bench overlooking a microcosm of the Los Angeles skyscape. We learn later it’s their favorite spot. As Summer places her hand over Tom’s, we notice the shiny diamond ring on her finger. Is she married to him, a viewer may ask. Probably not, as the film title implies, and as the narrator warns “This is not a love story.”

I like the movie as much as I liked it the first time. And like the first time, when presented with the question of why I like it, I don’t know the answer.
