Life is like a boxset from Fantasy Flight Games

Wednesday June 08, 2011

Living is like playing a board game. On a slow Monday Memorial day afternoon I was playing Ghost Stories with AG and GS. A recurring complaint among us is the play mechanics relies heavily on chance. Its also impossible to plan more than one round ahead. So we played with a set of new rules designed to make playing rely less on chance. With the new rules, we won more games. Actually, we stopped losing at all. It became very unlikely for players to lose rolls against Ghosts. The game stopped being fun. It was too predictable. I thought, is it impossible to make this game fun? Is there a Goldilocks region between randomness and predictability that we can tweak Ghost Stories to? Or is the game irreparable and we should chuck the board into the sea and move on to something else?

Oh, back to my original point. I think life is like a board game. In the way where you can tweak the dial between random and predictable. If you want to live with little to no surprises, you can simply stay at home, work the same job, live in the same city. This is totally fine. Some people prefer board games where there is little chance and they get to plan every step of the way. If you are a “the skies the limit” kind of person, you can go out every night, talk to random people, work different cities or countries. Thats cool too. There exists people who love Ghost Stories for its extreme reliance on chance and lack of future planning (and also enjoy losing).

Different Goldilocks region between chance and predictability exist for each person. But I would assume some universalities. Most people will want physical security. We want to be able to eat when we’re hungry. We want to rest in a warm place when we’re sleepy. We want to walk home to and from our car/bus/train without being attacked. There are many people who want these things and don’t have them. And there are many more who cannot afford to quit their predictable jobs.

For fortunate people secure in the things above, there is wiggle room to turn the dial one way or the other. If you’re unhappy with the way things are going for you, maybe you should. Can’t find a secure job? Dial down uncertainty by earning a college degree.

Special Item: College Diploma +N to social cachet, where N is amount paid for item divided by 10,000. Remove this item when you die.

Conversely, if you find living incredibly dull and life moves like the snails (damn those slimy slug-cousins) on my studio pathway, dial up. Go outside and cross dangerous streets. Talk to people you otherwise wouldn’t. Ask that girl from Macquarrie Hall 223 to coffee.
