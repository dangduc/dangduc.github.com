"You should treat others the way you want to be treated" is a saying television taught me when I was a young person. The Golden Rule helps guide people to 'do the right' thing. You shouldn't steal because you wouldn't want people to steal from you. And you should help old women walk across the street because you would want to be walked across the street, too. Er --that doesn't make sense. What if you're a young male in his early twenties. No way you'd need help walking across the street by an older woman. Unless she's a cougar and you're walking to a hotel. 

Sometimes, it doesn't make sense to treat others the way you'd like to be treated. Like that episode of *New Girl* when Nick gives Schmidt a six-pointed star-shaped cookie. Nick loves cookies, so he thought Schmidt would like a cookie, too. Schmidt states "[the cookie] is so terrible!" If you knew Schmidt, you'd know he overcame an eating disorder over the course of his post-college adulthood. So you can't treat people the way you'd want to be treated, because maybe they wouldn't want to be treated the same as you. Let's revise the Golden Rule to say "treat others the way they would want to be treated." This way, you can give cookies to people who want cookies, and non-cookies to people who don't want cookies. See, case close. 

Well, what if someone wants to eat tons of cookies. I mean, an unhealthy amount of cookies. According to the revised rule, it's okay to give this hypothetical person all the cookies he or she wants. Not a good idea. Let's revise again, and organize it like the three laws of robotics:

1. Do not cause harm to people or allow people to come to harm. 
1. Treat others the way *they* want to be treated unless it conflicts with the first law.
3. Treat others the way *you* want to be treated unless it conflicts with the first or second law.

*(note: in the last novel of the Foundation series, Asimov wrote about the '3 Laws of Humanics.')*

I think in general hand-wavy-ness, these three laws are *good enough*.
