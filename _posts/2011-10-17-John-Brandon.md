I attended a talk by John Brandon, VP of Apple's Marketing division. One of the things he talked about was..

## Have a Focus Group, Not a Fan Club

Whenever John needs to make a major decision, he gets the opinion of each and every one of a small and select group of trusted friends, including his wife. According to John, it is optimal for us to have such a group. 

## Motivation

How do we know when we are wrong, when we are only surrounded by Yes Men. John acknowledges that we can't do it all alone. We need people who can correct us. 

## Criteria

I believe there are a few criteria between pact-mates that must be mutually shared:

1. Respect
2. Support
3. Honesty
4. Trust
5. Care


### 1. Respect
Pact-mates must respect each other. Neither one is inferior. This means a pact-mate should not think they *are better* than the other. Let's consider a hypothetical Jon and Steve.

**Bad**: *"Steve, you should listen to what I say because I'm not as promiscuous as you."*

Jon believes Steve has a character flaw which prevents him from making good decisions. You shouldn't make a pact with someone you do not respect.

**Good**: 

*"Jon, I know you have many years of experience being a developer, but I want to do things my own way."*

*"Okay, Steve, I will respect your decision, even though I don't think it is the best approach. I'll be here for you if you need help."*

Jon makes his opinion known, but clearly shows support for Steve's decision, even though Jon's opinion goes against Steve's decision. Yes, it is possible to do this. This leads to the second criteria..

### 2. Support

Pact-mates help each other out. These are the people who will give you a ride at 3AM in the morning. Who will help you move out even though they are truly busy. Pact-Mates expect each other to pick up the phone and talk for an hour about their issues. These are the people who have your back even when they do not believe in your cause. 

Anti-example: one of the easiest ways to lose a friend is to complain about the friend's love-partner. Even if you know they should not be together, you have to suck it up and keep quiet; Because he might turn out to be her husband someday, and you are now incompatible with their situation. Be supportive of coupling.

### 3. Honest

Sometimes, it is okay to be silent. Don't volunteer your unflattering opinion of the new girlfriend if your pact-mate never asks. A general rule is tell the truth when you are directly asked: *"Do you like my new girlfriend?"* You may respond *"Nope."*
